# Main Tab 4: Performance (Quantitative Analytics)

## The Objective
This is where we flex the W3B Engine's historical success. Retail bettors just look at "Win/Loss". We look at Sharpe Ratios, Maximum Drawdown, and Alpha Decay. This tab proves that out system isn't luck—it's math.

## Language & Nomenclature Shift
- **❌ BANNED RETAIL TERMS:** Win Rate, Hot Streak, Bad Beat, Record.
- **✅ REQUIRED INSTITUTIONAL TERMS:** Sharpe Ratio, Alpha Generation, Variance, Drawdown, Yield Curve, Calibration Accuracy, Model Efficacy.

## Visual Implementation Plan
1. **The Yield Curve:**
   - A massive, smooth, glowing cyan bezier curve (using Lightweight Charts or Recharts) plotting our PnL over time. 
   - No grid lines on the chart, just raw data geometry floating over the deep navy WebGL fog.
2. **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
   - Four `<InstitutionalGlass />` KPI blocks at the top:
     - Total Alpha Generated
     - Max Drawdown (kept visually minimal)
     - Average Position Edge
     - Engine Calibration Score (% of times the model's assigned probability perfectly matched reality).
3. **Calibration Scatter Plot:**
   - A crucial visual for quants: comparing Market Probability (Kalshi) vs. W3B Assigned Probability. Plotting these data points interactively shows exactly where our algorithm consistently finds asymmetrical value against the public.
